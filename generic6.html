Panel Data and Binary Dependent Variables Analysis

In the analysis, two distinct sections are addressed. Firstly, under the Panel Data Model, the data is introduced, and the research question is outlined. A comprehensive descriptive analysis of variables is conducted, incorporating relevant figures with comments, particularly focusing on graphical depictions of individual heterogeneity. Three models—Pooled, Fixed Effects, and Random Effects—are fitted, and the preferred model is identified based on statistical diagnostics. The analysis involves thorough commentary on findings and justifications for the model selection.

Moving to the second section, Binary Dependent Variables, the data and research question are briefly discussed. The descriptive analysis involves histograms, fitted distributions, correlation plots, boxplots, scatterplots, and statistical summaries for the variables, including proportions for binary variables. Subsequently, three models—Linear Probability Model, Probit Model, and Logit Model—are fitted. The preferred model is identified, substantiated with statistical diagnostics, and accompanied by insightful commentary on the findings. The comprehensive approach aims to provide a robust understanding of the data and insights into the preferred modeling choices for each scenario.
