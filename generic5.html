Exploratory Data Analysis

This analysis is structured into three main sections. Firstly, the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is initiated by framing the question under investigation, citing the dataset, and providing a summary of its content. Completeness and consistency of the data are checked, and missing observations are addressed. Descriptive analyses, including histograms with overlaid density, boxplots, and cross-correlation, are conducted and accompanied by insightful comments.

The second section, Data PreProcessing, delves into assessing the stationarity of variables using time series plots, autocorrelation functions (ACF), and partial autocorrelation functions (PACF). The Dickey-Fuller test is employed to confirm stationarity and determine the order of differencing if needed. The process of differencing is executed, and the analyses are repeated on the differenced time series.

Moving to Feature Generation, Model Testing, and Forecasting, an autoregressive (AR) model is fitted using appropriate criteria like AIC. The autocorrelation function (ACF) of the residuals is scrutinized, and formal tests for autocorrelation are conducted. Subsequently, an autoregressive distributed lag (ARDL) model is employed, and the residual analysis is repeated.

The final steps involve summarizing findings, comparing model performances, and suggesting potential limitations or improvements based on statistical tests, such as degree of freedom restrictions and considerations for reverse causality. This comprehensive approach aims to provide a robust analysis and interpretation of the time series data.
