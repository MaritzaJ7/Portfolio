Cross-sectional Data Analysis

This analysis involves the development and refinement of a multiple linear regression model. Initially, the focus is on addressing a specific research question. The dataset is thoroughly described, including its source, content, and a comprehensive analysis of variables through histograms, correlation matrices, and five-number summaries. Potential violations of regression assumptions are considered. Subsequently, a baseline model is estimated, and individual estimates are scrutinized for both statistical and economic significance. The overall fit of the model is assessed, considering potential issues identified earlier. Feature selection is conducted, testing for multicollinearity using VIF and employing AIC or Schwartz Criterion for predictor subset determination. Residual analysis, RESET test, and tests for heteroskedasticity are performed and commented upon. A final model is developed, incorporating interaction terms or higher-order terms based on previous findings, and its performance is evaluated using model comparison criteria. The conclusion summarizes key findings, insights, and recommendations stemming from the comprehensive regression analysis.
