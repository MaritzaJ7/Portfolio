Site Suitability Analysis in QGIS

The ensuing analysis evaluates the optimal campground locations predicated on five pivotal factors: elevation, slope, proximity to major roads, distance to wildland fire stations, and vegetation cover type. Campsite desirability is intricately scored, with distinct criteria for each factor. Regarding elevation, sites below 4000ft garner a modest score of one point, escalating by one point for every 500ft increase, culminating in a maximum desirability score of five points for elevations surpassing 6000ft. In terms of slope, lower values denote heightened desirability, with a slope of 0 to 1 earning the highest score of five points and a slope greater than 10 earning the lowest score of 0 points. Proximity to major roads and fire stations follows a graded scale, with closer distances receiving higher desirability scores. Sites adjacent to major roads less than 1⁄4 mile away earn the highest score of 5 points, gradually diminishing with increasing distance. Similarly, locations within 1⁄4 mile of wildland fire stations earn the maximum desirability score of 5 points. Lastly, the presence of coniferous forest, mixed forest, or oak woodlands augments desirability. In short, campsite locations accumulating a cumulative score exceeding 15 points across all factors emerge as the most suitable for camping and are depicted on the map above.
